How to use this tool

A guide to using COMPASS.

1. Select Data Source

TrendWatch offers two distinct ways to generate analysis, depending on your needs:

  • Simulation (Generated Data): Select "Simulation" to have the tool automatically generate random numbers based on statistical parameters. This is ideal for theoretical testing where you want to see how a chart reacts to specific distributions (Normal, Lognormal) without needing an external file.
  • Real-World Data (CSV Upload): Select "Upload CSV" to import your own facility's historical data. The tool will calculate the baseline (mean and standard deviation) directly from your file, allowing you to validate methods against actual records.

2. Define Behavior (Simulation Only)

If you chose Simulation, you must define how the data behaves before any anomalies occur:

  • Stable: Data fluctuates around a constant mean (Normal or Lognormal distribution).
  • Trending: Data steadily increases or decreases over time (Linear).
  • Periodic: Data follows a sinusoidal wave pattern.

3. Induce Changes

Force the process to go "Out of Control" on a specific day to test detection speed:

  • Step Change: A sudden instantaneous jump. (e.g. Factor 1.2 = 20% increase).
  • Trending Change: A gradual drift that starts on the change day.
  • Amplitude Change: (Periodic only) The wave height increases/decreases.
  • Period Change: (Periodic only) The cycle frequency speeds up or slows down.

4. Analysis Methods

Shewhart Standard control limits. Highly effective for detecting large, sudden shifts in process behavior.
EWMA Exponentially Weighted Moving Average. Ideal for detecting small, subtle shifts.
MC-EWMA Markov Chain EWMA. An advanced variation that models state transitions for enhanced sensitivity.
CUSUM Cumulative Sum. Extremely sensitive to small, sustained deviations by accumulating errors over time.
GLM Generalized Linear Model. A powerful statistical approach that factors in historical baselines and underlying trends.
Farrington A specialized surveillance algorithm designed specifically for discrete count data.

5. Compare & Finalize (Operating Curve)

  • Reanalyze: After a run, click "Reanalyze" in the top menu to test a different method (e.g. switching from Shewhart to CUSUM) or parameter (e.g. changing λ from 0.2 to 0.4) on the exact same dataset.
  • Finalize & Compare (Operating Curve): Once you have generated multiple runs in your history, click the "Finalize & Compare" button. This takes you to the Operating Curve page.

    The Operating Curve runs a massive simulation to plot the Average Run Length (ARL) of your methods against various shift percentages. This creates a master graph allowing you to visually identify which algorithm and parameter configuration is most sensitive at specific anomaly sizes.
